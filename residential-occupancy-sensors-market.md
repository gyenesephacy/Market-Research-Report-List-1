<p><strong>The Residential Occupancy Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The residential occupancy sensors market consists of several types, including Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors, Ultrasonic sensors, and Dual-technology sensors. PIR sensors detect motion based on heat signatures emitted by occupants, making them energy-efficient. Ultrasonic sensors utilize sound waves to sense movement, offering greater coverage in complex spaces. Dual-technology sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies for heightened accuracy and reduced false alarms. Additionally, other sensors may include microwave and photoelectric systems, diversifying the options available for energy management in homes.</p></p>
