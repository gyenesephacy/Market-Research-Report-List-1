<p><p>Space-based broadband internet operates across various frequency bands, each suited for specific applications. The Ku, Ka, and K bands (12 GHz-40 GHz) support high-speed data transfer, benefiting consumer and enterprise broadband services. C and X bands (4 GHz-12 GHz) are commonly used for satellite communication, offering reliable links for television and internet services. L and S bands (1 GHz-4 GHz) are ideal for mobile satellite services, providing robust coverage and resilience, particularly in remote locations and emergency communications.</p></p>
