<p><p>Silk market types include Mulberry, Tussar, Eri, and Muga silk, each distinguished by their unique characteristics. Mulberry silk, the most common, is renowned for its softness and luster, produced by silkworms fed exclusively on mulberry leaves. Tussar silk, derived from wild silkworms, has a rough texture and rich golden hue. Eri silk, produced by Eri silkworms, is known for its warmth and durability. Muga silk, exclusive to Assam, is prized for its natural golden color and strength, making it highly sought after.</p></p>
